Web Usability

Web usability is about making your site easy for people to use: Is information easy to find? Are features easy to find? Is the navigation easy to understand? Is the process of buying goods clear and easy? Is it easy to register as a user? Can you easily recover a lost password?

Good standards of usability help to keep users on your site. Designers and developers need to remember that there are likely to be numerous other websites offer the same or a similar service, so if your site's usability is poor, visitors can easilty click to another site.

Web Accessibility

Web accessibility is about designing your website so that people with disabilities can have, as far as possible, the same experience when using your website as a person with no disabilities. It can include visual, auditory, physical, cognitive and neurological disibilities.

Under the 'Disability Discrimination Act 1995' Part III (Access to Goods & Services), there is a legal requirement on business to make their websites accessible. An accessible web can help people with disabilities participate more actively in society.
